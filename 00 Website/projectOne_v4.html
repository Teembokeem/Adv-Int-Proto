I happened to stumble on David King's Fractal Tree. It fit every requirement for the tree. I took the time to dissect and try to understand how he was able to achieve a natural tree. He was also able to add events to control virtually every aspect of the tree... I used this as the foundation for adding interactivity.

potential

As you can see, randomizing the angle as part of a recursive function, requires imprinting the directions per node. I couldn't figure out how to replicate the grow function into the render function, in order to backtrace the specific rotation and depth where the shape was created. This was further exacerbated by attempting to create PShapes in 3D space. Tree splaying was mapped to a potentiometer on a Particle Argon.
